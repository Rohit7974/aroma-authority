# What Are Fragrance Notes? Understanding the Secret Behind Every Great Scent

# Have you ever smelled a scented candle and instantly fallen in love with its fragrance, only to notice that the scent changes after a while? That’s not your imagination—it’s the magic of fragrance notes at work.

Whether you’re choosing a scented candle, room diffuser, car diffuser, or perfume, understanding fragrance notes can help you make better choices and discover scents you’ll truly enjoy.

At Siyaas, we believe that every fragrance tells a story. Just like a beautiful piece of music unfolds one note at a time, a well-crafted scent reveals different layers throughout your experience. These layers are known as fragrance notes.

In this guide, we’ll explain what fragrance notes are, how they work, and why they matter when selecting the perfect scented candle.

## What Are Fragrance Notes?

Fragrance notes are the individual scent layers that make up a complete fragrance.

Rather than smelling exactly the same from beginning to end, most premium fragrances are designed to evolve over time. Different ingredients become noticeable at different stages, creating a richer and more enjoyable scent experience.

These layers are divided into three categories:

Top Notes

Middle Notes (Heart Notes)

Base Notes

Together, they create what fragrance experts call a “fragrance pyramid.”

## The Fragrance Pyramid

Think of a fragrance as a story with a beginning, middle, and end.

### Top Notes – The First Impression

Top notes are the scents you notice immediately when you smell a candle or diffuser.

These notes are usually:

Fresh

Light

Bright

Quick to evaporate

Common Top Notes Include:

Lemon

Orange

Bergamot

Grapefruit

Mint

Eucalyptus

Top notes create the first impression of a fragrance and play a major role in attracting attention.

For example, when you first open a Siyaas Citrus Breeze candle, the refreshing citrus aroma you notice is likely coming from the top notes.

## Middle Notes – The Heart of the Fragrance

Once the top notes begin to fade, the middle notes emerge.

These are often considered the “heart” of the fragrance because they define its true character.

Common Middle Notes Include:

Lavender

Rose

Jasmine

Geranium

Cinnamon

Green Tea

Middle notes usually make up the largest portion of a fragrance blend and remain noticeable for the longest period.

If you’ve ever lit a candle and noticed a soft floral or calming aroma filling the room after a few minutes, you’re experiencing the heart notes.

## Base Notes – The Lasting Impression

Base notes appear after the fragrance has settled.

These notes are rich, deep, and long-lasting.

Common Base Notes Include:

Vanilla

Sandalwood

Cedarwood

Amber

Musk

Patchouli

Base notes provide depth and warmth while helping the fragrance last longer.

When a room still smells wonderful hours after extinguishing a candle, the base notes are often responsible.

## Why Fragrance Notes Matter in Scented Candles

Many people choose candles based solely on the label or packaging.

However, understanding fragrance notes helps you:

### Choose Scents You’ll Actually Enjoy

If you love fresh fragrances, look for candles with citrus and herbal top notes.

If you prefer relaxing aromas, floral heart notes like lavender and jasmine may be ideal.

If you enjoy warm, luxurious fragrances, candles with vanilla, amber, or sandalwood base notes are often excellent choices.

### Understand How a Candle Will Smell Over Time

A candle’s fragrance changes as it burns.

The scent you notice first may not be the scent you experience an hour later.

Knowing the fragrance notes helps set realistic expectations and prevents disappointment.

### Find Better Gifts

Understanding fragrance notes makes it easier to choose candles for friends and family.

For example:

Citrus notes appeal to most people.

Floral notes feel elegant and calming.

Vanilla and amber create a cozy atmosphere.

Sandalwood offers a sophisticated, premium feel.
