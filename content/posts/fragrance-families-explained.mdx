The human olfactory system is directly wired to the amygdala and hippocampus—the brain's emotional and memory centers. Because of this physiological connection, the scents we choose for our homes do not just mask odors; they design our emotional climate.

To curating a luxury environment, one must understand the classification of scent. In this guide, we decode the **Fragrance Wheel** and explain how to select and layer home fragrances to improve wellness.

## The Structure of the Fragrance Wheel

Created by taxonomist Michael Edwards, the Fragrance Wheel categorizes scents into four primary families, each subdivided into specific sub-families.

```
                  [Fragrance Wheel]
                         |
      +------------------+------------------+
      |                  |                  |
   [Floral]          [Oriental]          [Woody]           [Fresh]
      |                  |                  |                 |
 - Soft Floral      - Soft Amber       - Woods           - Citrus
 - Floral Oriental  - Amber            - Mossy Woods     - Green
 - Floral           - Woody Amber      - Dry Woods       - Water
```

### 1. The Floral Family
The most popular scent family, characterized by the aroma of freshly cut blossoms.
* **Sub-families:** Floral, Soft Floral, Floral Oriental.
* **Key Notes:** Rose, Jasmine, Peony, Gardenia.
* **Vibe:** Romantic, uplifting, elegant.

### 2. The Oriental (Amber) Family
Rich, exotic, and heavy scents that feature warm spices, resins, and sweet notes.
* **Sub-families:** Soft Amber, Amber, Woody Amber.
* **Key Notes:** Vanilla, Patchouli, Sandalwood, Cardamom, Myrrh.
* **Vibe:** Luxurious, sensual, comforting.

### 3. The Woody Family
Warm, earthy aromas that mimic forest floors, cut timber, and dry tree barks.
* **Sub-families:** Woods, Mossy Woods, Dry Woods.
* **Key Notes:** Cedarwood, Vetiver, Sandalwood, Pine.
* **Vibe:** Grounded, calm, sophisticated.

### 4. The Fresh Family
Effervescent, clean, and bright fragrances that smell of citrus, herbs, and ocean air.
* **Sub-families:** Citrus, Green, Water, Aromatic.
* **Key Notes:** Bergamot, Lemongrass, Sage, Mint, Eucalyptus, Sea Salt.
* **Vibe:** Energetic, clean, clarifying.

## The Fragrance Pyramid: Notes Explained

A balanced home scent is designed like a musical chord, composed of three tiers of volatility:

```
      /\
     /  \      Top Notes (10-15 mins) - e.g., Bergamot, Lemon
    /----\
   /      \    Heart Notes (1-2 hours) - e.g., Jasmine, Lavender
  /--------\
 /          \  Base Notes (4-8 hours) - e.g., Sandalwood, Vanilla
/____________\
```

When selecting home scents, always read the base notes. While citrus (top note) will greet you when you open the candle, the woody or amber base notes are what will linger in your room hours after the flame is snuffed.

## Olfactory Wellness: Designing Moods

Olfactory aromatherapy uses plant compounds to modulate cognitive response. Here is how to apply scent profiles room-by-room to support emotional wellness:

### The Living Room: Cozy Connection
* **Target:** Comfort, warmth, conversation.
* **Recommended Family:** **Woody Amber**.
* **Key Notes:** Sandalwood, Amber, Vanilla, Nutmeg.

### The Home Office: Cognitive Performance
* **Target:** Concentration, energy, memory retention.
* **Recommended Family:** **Citrus Fresh**.
* **Key Notes:** Lemongrass, Rosemary, Bergamot, Mint.
* *Note: Rosemary has been scientifically shown to enhance memory recall by up to 15%.*

### The Bedroom: Restful Slumber
* **Target:** Relaxation, anxiety reduction, melatonin support.
* **Recommended Family:** **Soft Floral & Herbal**.
* **Key Notes:** Lavender, Chamomile, Ylang-Ylang.

## The Art of Scent Layering

Scent layering is the practice of combining different home fragrances across your home to create a cohesive sensory journey.

1. **Keep it Consistent:** Avoid using conflicting heavy scents (e.g., burning a heavy chocolate amber candle next to a gardenia reed diffuser).
2. **The Transition Zone:** Use a light, unifying note (like Vanilla or Bergamot) in corridors to connect different room scents.
3. **Contrast and Balance:** Combine a clean, fresh scent (like eucalyptus) in the bathroom with a grounding woody scent (like cedarwood) in the master bedroom.
